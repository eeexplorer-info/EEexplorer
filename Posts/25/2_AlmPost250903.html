3OBSERVATION (SEEING DAD)

Something shared by an 'old timer' in the late 1980's about how we see Dad over time. Howard Goodfellow (rip) was turning 60 at the time.


At 5 - Dad knows everything!
At 7 - Dad knows a lot.


At 10 - Maybe Dad doesn't know everything.
At 12 - Hmm... Dad might be wrong sometimes.


At 14 - Dad doesn't get it!
At 16 - I can't always take Dad seriously!


At 18 - What does Dad even know?
At 22 - Dad's opinions feel outdated.


At 24 - I think I know more that Dad.
At 26 - Okay... Dad has some good points.


At 30 - Maybe I should ask Dad about this?
At 40 - How did Dad handle all of this so well?


At 45 - Turns out Dad was right about a lot.
At 50 - I wish I could still ask Dad. There's so much I've love to learn.


This could be a good guide for your projects or working with others by substituting months for years. For me, the increments have resonated well over the years.
