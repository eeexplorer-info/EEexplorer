"Rumi was a 13th-century Persian poet. Rumi's influence transcends national borders and ethnic divisions: Iranians, Tajiks, Turkics, Turks, Greeks, Pashtuns, other Central Asian and the Muslims of the Indian subcontinent, have greatly appreciated his spiritual legacy for the past seven centuries. 

His poems have been widely translated into many of the world's languages and transposed into various formats, described as the "most popular poet" and the "best selling poet" in the United States. 
His Masnavi, composed in Konya, is considered one of the greatest poems of the Persian language, like most of his work, but occasionally he also used Turkish, Arabic, and Greek in his verse. 
Rumi was also famous Sufi mystic, Hanafi faqih, Islamic scholar, Maturidi theologian..."

Born: 30 September 1207, Balkh (present-day Afghanistan), or Wakhsh (present-day Tajikistan), Khwarezmian Empire
Died: 17 December 1273 (aged 66), Konya (present-day Turkey), Sultanate of Rum
Resting place: Tomb of Mevlana Rumi, Mevlana Museum, Konya, Turkey
.
