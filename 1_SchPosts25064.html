064

KJ		 Big congratulations to Vijay Sivaraman, Hassan Habibi Gharakheili from EET and Ayyoob Hamza on the successful grant of their Australian patent: "Apparatus and process for detecting network security attacks on IoT devices."

This pioneering invention is among the first to create an AI-driven, real-time anomaly detection engine using the standardised IETF MUD framework to establish baseline IoT behaviour.

The model has been rigorously tested across diverse IoT devices and validated against a wide range of cyber-attacks, marking a significant step forward in IoT security.

The patent reflects the team's deep research at UNSW Engineering and is now being put into practice through Canopus Networks' AI-native Network Observability platform.

A major achievement and an important contribution to strengthening global IoT security.
